Gynecomastia Correction: A Comprehensive Overview

Gynecomastia correction is a specialized procedure designed to reduce excess breast tissue in men, which can cause enlarged or swollen male breasts. This condition, known as gynecomastia, can affect one or both breasts and is often caused by hormonal imbalances,weight gain, medications, drug abuse or genetics.  

It is important for many men to look and feel comfortable when swimming, gyming, exercising and wearing certain clothing. The procedure is performed to sculpt a more masculine chest, restoring self-confidence and body symmetry.

Dr. Rachana Tataria uses advanced surgical techniques to ensure natural-looking, long-lasting results, while also minimizing scars and promoting faster recovery. Whether you're dealing with a mild or severe case, gynecomastia correction can provide a transformative solution to achieve a flat, contoured chest.

What is gynecomastia, and how does it affect men?

Gynecomastia is the enlargement of male breast tissue, which may result in visible swelling or a feminized appearance of the chest. It is often caused by hormonal changes, weight gain, medication side effects, or genetic factors.

This condition can affect self-esteem and cause emotional distress, but the gynecomastia correction procedure can restore a more masculine chest contour.

What does gynaecomastia surgery involve?

The procedure involves removal of excess gland tissue which is often combined with liposuction to give better chest contouring.

You will have small incisions placed in discreet areas or creases for better long term scar outcomes. For men with excess skin we sometimes combine skin excision too.

Is gynecomastia correction surgery safe?

Yes, gynecomastia correction is a safe and effective procedure when performed by an experienced surgeon like Dr. Rachana Tataria.

She ensures the highest standards of care, reducing risks and providing natural, aesthetically pleasing results.

Can gynecomastia recur after surgery?

While gynecomastia correction typically provides permanent results, it is important to maintain a stable weight including healthy lifestyle and avoid factors that could cause hormonal imbalances.

If weight gain or medication usage leads to excess breast tissue, it may need further attention.

Is the surgery painful?

Most patients experience mild to moderate discomfort following the surgery, which can be managed with prescribed pain medication.

Dr. Rachana ensures the procedure is as comfortable as possible and will guide you through the recovery process to minimize pain.

Health Benefits of Gynecomastia Correction

Gynecomastia correction not only enhances physical appearance but also offers significant emotional and psychological benefits. Men who undergo this procedure often experience a boost in self-confidence and body image.

The correction can lead to improved comfort, especially when performing physical activities or wearing certain clothing. It can also alleviate the emotional strain caused by the self-consciousness that often accompanies gynecomastia, leading to an overall sense of well-being.

How does gynecomastia correction improve self-esteem?

For many men, enlarged breasts can lead to feelings of embarrassment or discomfort in social situations.

Gynecomastia correction reshapes the chest, helping to restore a more masculine silhouette, which often results in improved self-confidence and a more positive body image.

Are there any long-term health benefits from the procedure?

In addition to improving appearance, gynecomastia correction can relieve physical discomfort associated with enlarged breasts, such as tenderness or irritation caused by tight clothing.

Many patients find that their ability to exercise and engage in physical activities improves as well.

Can gynecomastia correction help with emotional distress?

Yes, many patients report feeling much more at ease socially and emotionally after the surgery.

By correcting the physical appearance, it addresses the psychological distress that comes with the condition, helping individuals feel more confident in their daily life.

Procedure of Gynecomastia Correction

The gynecomastia correction procedure is performed under local anesthesia with sedation or general anesthesia, depending on the complexity of the case.

Dr. Rachana begins by making small incisions around the areola or in discreet areas of the chest to remove excess gland tissue and reshape the breast.
‍
In many cases, liposuction may be used to remove excess fat, ensuring a smoother and more contoured chest. The procedure usually lasts about 1 to 2 hours, and Dr. Rachana ensures minimal scarring for optimal aesthetic results.

How long does the gynecomastia correction surgery take?

The surgery typically lasts between 1 and 2 hours, depending on the extent of tissue removal required.

Dr. Rachana will provide a detailed timeline during your consultation based on your individual needs.

Are the incisions visible after surgery?

Dr. Rachana uses precise techniques to minimize visible scarring, typically making incisions around the areola or in inconspicuous areas.

The scars usually fade over time, becoming less noticeable as healing progresses.

Do I need to stay in the hospital after the surgery?

Most gynecomastia correction procedures are performed on a daycase basis, meaning you can return home the same day.

However, you will need someone to drive you home and assist you during the first 24 hours after surgery.

Recovery Process of Gynecomastia Correction

After the surgery, patients typically experience swelling, bruising, and mild discomfort, which can be managed with pain medications prescribed by Dr. Rachana. Wearing a compression garment helps reduce swelling and supports the healing process.
‍
Most men can return to work within 1 to 2 weeks, although more strenuous activities should be avoided for about 4 to 6 weeks.

Follow-up visits are scheduled to ensure proper healing and monitor progress.

How long will it take to recover from gynecomastia correction surgery?

Most patients recover within 1 to 2 weeks, with the initial swelling and bruising subsiding during this time.

Full recovery may take 4 to 6 weeks, with a gradual return to normal activities.

What should I expect during the recovery process?

You will experience some swelling and bruising immediately after surgery, but these symptoms should improve over the course of the first few weeks.

Dr. Rachana will provide you with detailed aftercare instructions to promote a smooth recovery and achieve the best results.

Should I wear pressure garments after gynaecomastia surgery?

Yes, you should wear a pressure garment or a customised vest for at least 4-6 weeks following the procedure as it helps support the tissues for swelling and bruising and also aids in fat redraping.

The compression garment needs to be worn for 24 hours a day except for bathing.

When can I resume physical activities after gynecomastia correction?

Light activities like walking can be resumed within the first week.

More strenuous exercises, such as weightlifting or running, should be avoided for 4 to 6 weeks to ensure proper healing.

Will the results of gynecomastia correction be permanent?

Yes, the results are generally permanent, as excess breast tissue is permanently removed during the surgery.

However, maintaining a stable weight and avoiding certain medications can help maintain the results long-term.

When will I start seeing the results of gynaecomastia correction?

There will be mild swelling and bruising immediately after the surgery which takes 2-3 weeks to settle down.

You might have unevenness in the first few weeks which gradually will settle down and as your tissues continue to heal, it takes 3-6 months for final results to achieve.

Can gynecomastia correction help with asymmetrical breasts?

Yes, gynecomastia correction can address asymmetry by removing or redistributing tissue to create a more balanced appearance.

Dr. Rachana will ensure your chest appears symmetrical and well-proportioned after surgery.

Are there any risks involved with gynaecomastia surgery?

Like any other surgical procedure, there are risks of scarring, numbness, infection, bleeding, seroma or fluid collection and rarely nipple damage.

Dr Tataria will go through individual risks in detail during your consultation.
